  Local Lowdown 
​
🛍️ T.J. Maxx is coming to Liberty Hill this August
The Gist. T.J. Maxx signed on for a 25,304 square foot store at the Liberty Hill Crossing shopping center on N. US 183, with construction expected to wrap by mid August. It joins a wave of major retail landing nearby, including Target and Walmart anchors.
Why it matters. Right now the closest T.J. Maxx is over in Georgetown or Cedar Park, so for those of us out here that is one less reason to make the drive. Liberty Hill keeps picking up the stuff we used to leave town for, and honestly it is about time.

🛒 Cedar Park's Whole Foods is expanding by 8,000 square feet
The Gist. The Whole Foods Market at the 183A Toll center in Cedar Park is expanding by roughly 8,000 square feet, taking over adjacent storefronts including a former Talbots Outlet. The store will stay open during the renovation.
Why it matters. It is the anchor grocery for a lot of Cedar Park and south Leander, so a bigger footprint means more selection without anyone losing access during the work.

  Restaurant Radar 
​
The Cedar Park food truck that grew into a Texas Top 100 spot
Aleida's started as a little Venezuelan food truck on Bell Boulevard and earned such a following that the family opened this brick and mortar spot, and it has since landed on Yelp's Top 100 Places to Eat in Texas.
The arepas are the thing to order, those plush cornmeal pockets stuffed with shredded beef, black beans, and sweet plantains. Regulars also swear by the tequeños, the crispy cheese filled sticks, and the patacones for the table.
It stays relaxed, reasonably priced, and genuinely family run, the kind of place where the staff remembers your usual. Open daily from 9am, so it works for a weekend breakfast arepa or a weeknight dinner.
